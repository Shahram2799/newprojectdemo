Monteriggioni / Villa

In addition to 100% Full Synchronization, you can also fully 100% complete the Villa in Monteriggioni by upgrading shops, completing renovations, completing all collections and purchasing all equipment. When you buy shops and renovations and collect collectibles and equipment, you will cause the value of Monteriggioni and its income to increase. If you focus on 100% synchronizing with Assassin's Creed II first, then all you would have to do additionally is just upgrade every shop to level 3, complete all renovations, and purchase all equipment. The villa's income will generate for every 20 minutes that you play Assassin's Creed II, and Claudia will store the Villa's income in a chest next to her at the office in Monteriggioni, so make sure to collect it regularly. You can also speak to Claudia to consult Monteriggioni's balance sheets and track your progress towards 100% completion of Monteriggioni.

Achievements and Trophies

The following achievements and trophies can be achieved during multiple playthroughs of the game. These are discussed in a separate section as these are may or may not naturally happen during first playthrough of the game. Trophies and Achievements can also be obtained during free roam or after completion of the main game.

Myth Maker

Find the 8 statuettes in Monteriggioni.

There are a total of eight statuettes of Roman gods scattered throughout the town of Monteriggioni. Collect them all and place them onto the different pedestals surrounding the Villa Auditore to activate a sort of mechanism and collect 2000 florins for each pedestal. You can see here a guide on where to find all statuettes.

Vitruvian Man

Unlock all 20 pieces of Subject 16's video.

There are a total of twenty glyphs you can find scattered all around all regions of Italy that Ezio can interact with by using Eagle Vision. You have to solve a puzzle for each glyph, and the good thing is because the puzzles follow a set sequence, they arent tied to specific glyphs and you can find the glyphs in any order you want. Each glyph will unlock a piece of Subject 16's video, which you can access through the Animus Desktop menu. Collect them all to reveal Subject 16's video in its entirety. You can see here a guide on where to find all glyphs.

Street Cleaner

Hide 5 dead bodies in a Bale of Hay.

It's as simple as that. You can find and kill any 5 guards or NPCS, then use the interact button to carry their body and bring them all to the same haystack and hide their body altogether. Make sure to avoid being seen!

Fly Swatter

Kick a Guard while using the Flying Machine.

This trophy can be easily missable and can be obtained during Sequence 8 Memory 5, "Infrequent Flier", or through the Special Memory located in Romagna / Forli. While using the flying machine, you can kick many guards but need only one for the trophy. It is easy to do however, you have to be very careful because it's very hard to control the flying machine at a low altitude.

Messer Sandman

Stun 4 guards at once by throwing sand in their face.

This trophy can be hard to get but is very doable. To throw sand, you have to hold the attack button and when it is fully charged release it. You can learn this move from the trainer on the fighting grounds in Monteriggioni if you are up to it. Then find any 4 guards in any highly populated region such as Venice, there are usually groups protecting areas with hidden codex pages. You have to hit at least 4 guards with sand at once altogether to get the trophy.

Doctor

Perform an Air Assassination on a Poisoned NPC.

It's as simple as that. You unlock the ability to poison your enemies after Sequence 5 Memory 2, "A Blade with Bite", which you can access from your weapon wheel. Select the ability, find an enemy or NPC to poison near an elevated area which you can easily climb, poison the enemy then climb over the elevated area to perform an air assassination.

No-hitter

Kill 10 enemies while remaining in conflict without being hit.

This trophy may come naturally as you engage in swordfights with enemies throughout the game. An easy way to avoid getting hit is by spamming the counter kill ability and dodging and evading from time to time if you want to fight with true honor. Or if you want to be a p***y, you can also use smoke bombs during combat, which you unlock after Sequence 5 memory 7, "The Cowl does not make the Monk", and then kill them swiftly as they won't be able to move.

Kleptomaniac

Pickpocket 1000 Florins.

This trophy may come naturally as you pickpocket the citizens and guards of Italy. To pickpocket, you can just walk by a person and hold the interact button. You can keep going until you reach the needed amount. Or if you want to get it quicker, make sure you have some notoriety level, then bribe a herald then pickpocket them straight after to easily get 500 florins. You would need to bribe & pickpocket 2 heralds for this strategy.

Lightning Strike

Sprint for 100 meters.

It's as simple as that. This trophy may come naturally as you sprint across the grounds and rooftops of all regions of Italy. Run, Ezio, Run!!!

Sweeper

Sweep 5 guards at once by using a Long Weapon.

This trophy can be hard to get but is very doable. To use a sweep attack, while holding a long weapon, you have to hold the attack button and when it is fully charged release it. You can learn this move from the trainer on the fighting grounds in Monteriggioni if you are up to it. Then find a long ranged weapon by disarming a guard. Find a group of 5 guards (you can find them at the palace in Venice), and use the sweep attack, or if you want to make it easier, you can use a smoke bomb to blind them first then pick up the weapon & sweep them.

Show your Colors

Wear the Auditore cape in each city.

To get this trophy, you have to first make sure you have collected all 100 feathers and hae given them to Maria to obtain the Auditore Cape. Once you have the Cape, put it on through the main menu then visit all of the regions and mountains of Italy. Be careful though, having the Auditore Cape put on will make you famous and not in a good way...

Mailman

Intercept a Borgia Courrier.

This trophy may come naturally as you find borgia courriers running all around the different regions of Italy. Borgia Courriers always carry a lot of florins, so it's good to loot them early on to gain some income. Find a Borgia Courrier (they are typically dressed in red and will show on the map with a red icon), chase and tackle them to get the trophy.

Tip of the Iceberg

Use your Eagle Vision to scan a Glyph in the environment.

This trophy may come naturally as you find all 20 glyphs for the "Petruvian Man" trophy. You can scan any one glyph for the first time with your eagle vision to easily get the trophy.

A Piece of the Puzzle

Unlock a piece of Subject 16's video.

This trophy may come naturally as you find all 20 glyphs & solve each puzzle for the "Petruvian Man" trophy. You can scan any one glyph you find for the first time with your eagle vision, and solve the puzzle to unlock your first piece of Subject 16's video to easily get the trophy.

Perfect Harmony

Tint your clothes with those colors: Wetland Ebony and Wetland Ivory.

It's as simple as that. You can visit the Tailor Shop in Romagna / Forli as these clothing items are only available over there. Buy and equip these two colors to get the trophy.

Red Light Addict

Spend 5000 florins on Courtesans.

This trophy may come naturally as you continuously hire courtesans to hide yourself in plain sight from enemies or as you follow enemies into secluded areas. Courtesans are almost everywhere in Italy, so it's not too difficult to find them roaming around. It costs 150 florins to hire courtesans, so you have to keep spending money on them to get the trophy. There is no shortcut around this unfortunately.

Man of the People

Toss more than 300 florins on the ground.

It's as simple as that. Equip your money bag in the weapon wheel, and throw money near a group of people to give away 10 florins. Just keep throwing until you make those poor b**tards happier.

Victory lies in preparation

Get all Hidden Blades, Item Pouches and Armor upgrades for Ezio.

This trophy may come naturally as you continuously upgrade Ezio's arsenal to make him more powerful. Ezio will get all of the Hidden Blade's upgrades after Sequence 9 Memory 1, "Knowledge is Power". You can purchase all Item pouches for Ezio at any Tailor, and all Armor Upgrades at any Blacksmith. Do note that not all item pouches and armor upgrades will be available to buy until you further complete the main story, so it's recommended to purchase the upgrades you need and then complete the story to unlock every upgrade.

VIEWPOINTS

You can synchronize with a viewpoint in Assassin's Creed II to reveal the map and its surrounding areas. To synchronize with a viewpoint, climb the building to reach it and press the interact button upon climbing. You can perform a leap of faith off a viewpoint to easily get back to ground. Synchronizing with all viewpoints in all regions of Italy is required for 100% Synchronization. It is recommended to synchronize viewpoints as soon as you enter a new region to make navigation and mission / collectible finding easier.

SECRET LOCATIONS

There are 9 Secret Locations in Assassin's Creed II, which are namely Assassin Tombs and Templar Lairs.

After visiting the lair with Mario at the beginning of Sequence 4, you will find the Armor of Altair, which is locked behind a grate, which is fixed in place by six locks. These six locks can be unlocked by placing an Assassin Seal into each of the pedestals, which can be found by exploring Assassin Tombs. The 6 Assassin Tombs will be available to play after Sequence 4 Memory 4, "Novella's Secret", which you have to explore to retrieve the Assassin hidden within. In order to achieve perfect 100% synchronization with Ezio, you have to find all six Assassin Seals from each Assassin Tomb and obtain the Armor of Altair in the lair at Monteriggioni. The 3 Templar Lairs are part of the Deluxe Edition of the game, which can be explored to find hidden Templar treasures within.

FEATHERS

You can start to collect all 100 feathers scattered all throughout the regions of Italy after completing Sequence 1 Memory 7, "Petruccio's Secret". However, after completing Sequence 3 Memory 3, "Practice Makes Perfect", you will get the objective to go check on Ezio's mother, Maria. From this point on, you can start to place all collected feathers into the chest in Maria's room in Monteriggioni for 100% synchronization. After collecting and depositing 50 feathers inside the chest, Mario will unlock a new weapon, the Condottiero War Hammer, for you at the Villa's blacksmith. After collecting and depositing all 100 feathers inside the chest, Maria will gift Ezio with the Auditore Cape. But be warned, wearing the Cape outside Monteriggioni will cause Ezio to become notorious immediately...

TREASURES

You can loot treasure boxes in Assassin's Creed II to gain some florins. You can reveal treasure chests on the map by going to every art merchant in each region and district of Italy and buying the treasure maps that will mark the locations of the chests in the entire district. You have to loot all 330 treasure chests spread over the cities and mountains of Italy (even those hidden ones in the secret locations!) for 100% Synchronization.

You can visit the Villa's architect standing right next to Claudia in her office in Monteriggioni to order architectural upgrades. The architect has a price list for new shops and renovations, so you will have to bring him some florins and make a choice on which shop you want to upgrade or which renovation you want to complete and he will do it for you. If the architect builds you a shop, Ezio can purchase goods there at lower rates. If you invest more money in the shop, you will get an even greater discount.
