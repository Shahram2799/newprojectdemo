Jumpstart your criminal empire with the Career Builder and the Criminal Enterprises Starter Pack

Because of the GTA Career Builder that was introduced with the GTA V Enhanced Edition and the GTA premium online edition that comes with the criminal enterprises starter pack, Rockstar has given many GTA online players the advantage of already owning properties, businesses, vehicles and more. It is highly recommended to save money and keep your pockets as tight as possible by purchasing only what is necessary; you don't have to own or buy everything to have fun.

Player versus Player (PvP)

PvP activities consider all those activities that pit players against each other, which are also called competitive modes. These all range from deathmatches, races, parachuting, adversary modes, etc. The rewards from these activities can be really rewarding, especially during the weeks events where rewards are doubled or tripled. However, if you are a low level without experience, it is best to avoid these activities for now until you gain enough experience.

Player versus Environment (PvE)

PvE activities are essentially the contact missions , where the game pits you against enemy NPC's and can earn you around $5K - $15K. How you get paid from the contact missions depends entirely on how long it takes you to complete the mission.

When & What to Buy

So far, we have talked about the different ways we can make money in GTA online. However, once you actually start making money, how to use the money becomes the key to success.

Character Customization

First of all, do not go crazy and spend most of your money on high-end clothes , fancy haircuts , and expensive tattoos , unless you actually want to customize your character. There's nothing wrong with wanting to customize your character and expand your wardrobe; it is simply just not wise to use all of your money on them. There are far more valuable investments you can make with your money, and while it is okay to make your character look presentable in free mode, you just have to be smart with your money and only buy what is needed rather than what is wanted.

Weapons

As far as weapons go, you can do almost everything with 4 simple weapons.

  • Any pistol with a supressor
  • SMG with an extended clip
  • Special Carbine with a drum magazine and grip
  • Any kind of explosive weapon

You can also spend your money on other weapons that may be important or appealing to you that can help in free mode, PvP modes, and PvE jobs. You can also use weapon workshops throughout many owned properties to upgrade your regular weapons to mark II and make them more powerful. But again, you have to be smart with your money and only buy the needed weapons as you can still have fun in GTA online without fancy and expensive weapons.

Warehouse

Owning an Executive Office will grant you access to the SecuroServ Special Cargo Network, where you can start special cargo, vehicle cargo, and special vehicle missions. You can launch Special Cargo missions for your Special Cargo Warehouses . When you log on the network for the first time, you will have to purchase a warehouse to store special cargo. There are three different types of Warehouses: small, medium and large. Each Warehouse can store a different amount of crates. The maximum amount of Warehouses a player can purchase is five.

To get started with the special cargo business, make sure you own a CEO Office then log onto the Securoserv network on your office computer to first purchase any warehouse you want. The whole concept of the business is that you buy crates of product, collect the crate and bring it back to your warehouse, and sell it for more money.

You can source either 1 crate, 2 crates, or up to 3 crates at the same time. If you want to make the most money possible per crate, you would want to buy 1 crate at a time. The reason why you are given the option to buy 3 at the same time is because it is more efficient even though collecting the crates is more time consuming. If you want to make the most money possible, then definitely choose to buy 3 crates at the same time because it will bring in more profit when sold.

Once you have sourced enough cargo to store in the Warehouse, it is time to sell your cargo and earn a profit. Final Tips & Tricks

To wrap things up, here are a few tips and tricks to help make your experience playing GTA Online more simple and easy:

  • For every 48 minutes that you play GTA Online, the game will take money from you in what is called Daily Fees based on the properties that you own. This number can vary based on the number of properties that you own, as each property incurs a different value. To avoid paying this fee altogether, you can keep switching sessions every 40 minutes or so as the time counter resets every time you switch a session.
  • You can go for Career Progress challenges for any update that was brought to GTA Online if you want to make even more money and RP: it is recommended to do so for advanced players who play the game daily.
  • If you purchased the Criminal Enterprises starter pack or the GTA Online 2022 version with the Career Builder, ALWAYS take advantage of it and get the free vehicles and properties that Rockstar has gifted to you. It would save a lot of time, effort and money, and apart from the Paleto Bay bunker (the only free property you should avoid getting), every single property can help you in the long run.
  • A lot of people complain playing heists without friends and with random players in their lobbies is too tiresome and a terrifying experience. Although this may be true, this does not always have to be the case: there are people everywhere online willing to play with you. You just have to look for them in the right places, whether it be friends, players you meet online who are doing the same exact thing you are (making money), and players you meet on social media platforms who are willing to help you.
  • When you are playing a heist with a crew, you should always set the cut to give yourself a little more than the crew to make it more fair as they get paid for each of the setup missions. When playing with two players, always go for a 60% - 40% cut. If three players, then go for a 50% - 25% - 25% cut. 4 players, make it a 40% - 20% - 20% - 20% cut.
  • When you are completing heist prep missions freemode and any kind of mission that takes place in freemode (other than business sell missions), you should always try to play in an invite only session via settings so you can avoid griefers and angry GTA players looking for trouble.
  • When you are buying or selling any kind of business stock, you would always have to register as a VIP, CEO, or MC President. It is okay to register as any role, but when you sell Bunker stock with associates, you should register as a MC President so your associates earn more money as opposed to what they would earn if you were to register as a VIP or CEO.
  • You should always aim to buy supplies for your businesses rather than steal the supplies, unless you are short on cash. Buying the supplies will always fill up the stock progress fully, which is therefore the safest way to gain business goods. Or you could just raid Stash Houses daily as it would fill up the stock progress on any one of your businesses without you having to pay a single thing!
  • You should ALWAYS sell your business goods, whatever business it may be, in a public lobby. You would get a 1% bonus for each player in the lobby, up to 20%, for when you complete a sell mission. For example, if there are 30 players in a public lobby, and you are selling $500,000 worth of business stock, you would get an extra $100,000.
  • Keep an eye out for weekly events... you never know what business could go on sale and which business activity could go on double money! GTA+ could also give some benefits on businesses from time to time...
